Ambulance services — operational insights P1–P4 · 283K cases · response performance · FY23–24
Response time performance vs 90% target
All priority classes improving — none at target yet
P1 emergency P2 urgent P3 non-urgent 90% target
P1: 79.5→86.3%, P2: 72.5→78.9%, P3: 70.5→80.1% — all below 90% target.
FY23–24 actual vs 90% contractual target
P2 has the largest gap — 11.1pp below target
Actual performance 90% target
P1: 86.3% vs 90%, P2: 78.9% vs 90%, P3: 80.1% vs 90%.
Demand growing faster than performance
Emergency cases grew 6.8% in FY24 to 283,349 — but P1 performance improved only 3.3pp. If demand continues at this rate, the contractual target gap will widen again in FY25.
P2 gap is structural — 4+ years to target
P2 at 78.9% is 11.1pp below the 90% target. At the current +2.9pp/year improvement rate, the target won't be reached until FY2027–28 — the largest contractual performance risk under the ASA.
P3 improvement signals effective intervention
P3 improved +6.5pp in FY24 and +9.6pp over two years — the strongest recovery of any priority class. Isolating the drivers would identify which interventions are replicable for P2.
Triple Zero crossed 300K for the first time
307,689 Triple Zero calls in FY24, up 5.5%. This is the upstream driver for every operational metric — the highest-leverage figure to forecast for roster and fleet planning.
▶ Analyst recommendations — ambulance services
  1. Build a demand-vs-capacity model projecting P1/P2 performance under 4%, 6% and 8% YoY demand growth scenarios — gives senior leaders early warning of contractual breach risk.
  2. Correlate high-ramping days at Royal Perth and Fiona Stanley with P1/P2 response time misses to quantify per-site capacity impact.
  3. Develop a rolling 12-week Triple Zero call volume forecast as the primary lead indicator for roster planning and fleet deployment.

FY23–24 FY22–23
Employee expenses: $314M→$380M. Bad debts: $37.2M→$42M. Property: $23M→$28.3M.
Bad debt hit $42M — 8% of revenue
Bad and doubtful debts grew 12.7% to $41.98M, reflecting cost of living pressure on ambulance fee collection. Understanding which communities drive the highest write-off rates is both a financial and equity question.
Net surplus fell 83% despite 15% revenue growth
Revenue grew to $528M but surplus fell from $16.2M to $2.8M as employee costs jumped 21% (+$66M) and capital investment tripled to $62M. The organisation is in a heavy investment cycle with a thin operating buffer.

▶ Analyst recommendations — station coverage
  1. Validate zone gap estimates against internal station-level dispatch records — cases/station/month filtered to P1 and P2 directly answers where new capacity would reduce the P2 gap most efficiently.
