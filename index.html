Ambulance services — operational insights P1–P4 · 283K cases · response performance · FY23–24
Hover any point for exact values · dashed line = 90% contractual target
Hover bars to compare — P2 has the largest gap at 11.1pp below target
Demand growing faster than performance
Emergency cases grew 6.8% in FY24 to 283,349 — but P1 performance improved only 3.3pp. If demand continues at this rate, the target gap will widen again in FY25.
P2 gap is structural — 4+ years to target
P2 at 78.9% is 11.1pp below the 90% target. At +2.9pp/year, the target won't be reached until FY2027–28 — the largest contractual risk under the ASA.
P3 strongest recovery
P3 improved +9.6pp over two years — the strongest recovery of any priority class. Isolating drivers would identify which interventions are replicable for P2.
Triple Zero crossed 300K
307,689 Triple Zero calls in FY24, up 5.5% — the primary upstream driver for every operational metric and the highest-leverage figure to forecast.
▶ Analyst recommendations
  1. Build a demand-vs-capacity model projecting P1/P2 under 4%, 6% and 8% YoY demand growth scenarios — gives senior leaders early warning of contractual breach risk.
  2. Correlate high-ramping days at Royal Perth and Fiona Stanley with P1/P2 response time misses to quantify per-site impact.
  3. Develop a rolling 12-week Triple Zero forecast as the primary lead indicator for roster and fleet planning.

Hover bars · Ramping share grew from 12% (2022) to 18% (FY23–24)
Winter surge predictable 3 months out
June–August demand consistently 15–20% above average. Enables proactive workforce surge planning ahead of the peak.
Peak windows + ramping = worst-case scenario
8–10am and 6–9pm peaks combined with ramping events represent maximum response capacity degradation — shift-overlap analysis would quantify exposure.
Financial efficiency — cross-domain insights $528M revenue · $42M bad debt · $62M capex · FY23–24
Hover bars to compare · ASA revenue more than doubled year on year
Hover bars · Employee costs up $66M (+21%) — fastest growing expense line
Bad debt hit $42M — 8% of revenue
Bad and doubtful debts grew 12.7% reflecting cost of living pressure on ambulance fee collection. Postcode-level segmentation would identify the most affected communities.
Net surplus fell 83% despite 15% revenue growth
Revenue grew to $528M but surplus fell from $16.2M to $2.8M as employee costs jumped 21% and capex tripled to $62M. Organisation is in a heavy investment cycle with a thin buffer.
First aid training — service insights 307K engagements · revenue vs volume divergence · FY23–24
Bars = engagements (left axis) · Line = revenue (right axis) · Hover for exact values
Volume fell 11.6% while revenue grew 16%
A shift toward higher-value commercial courses at the expense of community volume. Is community access to first aid training declining? Decomposing by charitable vs commercial would answer this.
200K+ First Responder App downloads
Over 200,000 Western Australians have downloaded the First Responder app. Usage patterns, AED activations and geographic distribution are an untapped analytics asset.
